Design Value:

Minimalism:

The minimalist approach of this design is defined by no textual elements, I wanted the interface to rely only on images and icons. Each button uses emojis that clearly show its meaning without needing words. This makes the layout simple and keeps things visually clean.


The large looping background video helps avoid empty white spaces and creates a full, engaging screen. All the UI panels, like video and sound options, sit neatly on top of the video. By keeping these controls inside panels, the main interface stays uncluttered and lets users focus on the timer.


I also used grey tones and added transparency to buttons and sections. This softens the overall look, reduces harsh color contrast, and keeps the design light and minimal.


Immersion:

The looping background videos are designed to feel like real spaces, such as the rain falling, caf√© corners, forest paths, or city roads. These visuals, paired with layered ambient sounds like wind, birds, rain, or thunder, create a realistic and immersive soundscape.


The smooth animation of the hidden panel helps users stay in that immersive mood, since the transitions don‚Äôt break the flow. The transparent analog clock and timer also blend naturally with the videos, keeping the visuals seamless and avoiding distractions.


Personalization:

Users can choose different looped videos for their background, for example, a bedroom, caf√©, forest, living room, or snow house, depending on what mood they want.


They can also mix and match ambient sounds to create their own soundscape, layering different elements together to fit their personal taste.


The timer is fully adjustable, so users can set their own focus time in minutes. It shows both minutes and seconds for more accuracy.

Rationale - Design Principle

Feedback

Sound Control

The icon button changes to show the mute and unmute states: üîá with a red cross represents muted, and üîä means unmuted. The slider controls the volume of each individual sound track. Since there are multiple tracks, the sliders do not display numbers. The position of the sliders provides visual feedback of the current volume level. This intentional design ensures the sound will not startle users when it begins playing. Additionally, a pop-up reminder appears when entering the website to notify users about the sound.


Date/Time

The analog clock provides continuous visual feedback of time passing. The thickness of the clock hands differentiates the hour, minute, and second. Below the analog clock, there is also a numerical date and time, which provides textual feedback. This display corresponds to real-life time, reassuring users that the system is accurate. This aspect is extremely useful for Macbook users, as when entering full screen mode, the top bar will automatically be hidden, and to check the time Macbook users will have to move their mouse. It avoids users from breaking the immersion.


Timer

The countdown timer updates every second, providing an accurate and familiar way to display remaining time. When the timer ends, a pop-up alert gives explicit feedback to notify the user that the countdown has finished. The two control buttons (‚Äúplay/pause, restart‚Äù) allow the user to change the behavior of the timer at any stage, ensuring flexibility during the timing process.


Video Panel

A video panel is included to let users choose any background footage they like. By clicking the buttons, each one loads a different looping video scene, providing visual feedback that the environment has changed. Double-clicking a video button closes the panel to its minimal stage, giving users control over its visibility.


Information Design

The information design in my project includes buttons, sliders, and visual displays.

  • Buttons are designed with attention to size, color, and alignment. The color scheme is harmonious with the background videos, and the sizing ensures readability, hierarchy, and visual aesthetics.
  • Sliders are designed with the same considerations but specifically communicate range from minimum to maximum. Sliding left lowers the volume and sliding right raises it, following conventions familiar across many cultures.
  • Visual Displays include three major elements: the date/time, the background video, and the overall layout of the panels. Important techniques I used in my information design include size, color, alignment, and the use of positive and negative space.

Mapping

In the ‚ÄúStudy Space‚Äù section, mapping is clearly demonstrated through the sliders and buttons:

  • Dragging the slider to the right increases the volume in direct proportion to the percentage shown, making the interaction intuitive and easy to navigate.
  • Video switching happens quickly and accurately without errors, meaning the screen responds directly to the user‚Äôs input. This close relationship between controls (input) and outcomes (function) ensures accurate mapping.

Significant Changes:

Original Interface

I have made changes around the video panel, sound panel, timer panel and the Analog clock.

Video Section

The most significant change I made to the Video Panel is adding a sliding feature. The panel now only appears when the video button is clicked, sliding in from the right side of the interface. This keeps the layout clean and prevents the panel from taking up too much space on the main screen.


I also adjusted the size of each button and added clearer hover feedback. When users hover over an icon, the background color changes and the button slightly enlarges. This makes it easier for users to see which button they‚Äôre interacting with and navigate the panel more intuitively.


I decided to remove one of the ‚ÄúBed‚Äù looped videos because I didn‚Äôt want to have repeated icons. It was hard to choose between the two, but I ended up keeping the darker scene as the default background video. The most significant change I made is the time display in the timer section. I moved the numerical display outside of the box and made it very large, with transparency so it blends with the background while keeping both elements visible.

For the Analog Clock, since it‚Äôs a key element to showcase my ‚Äúskeuomorphism‚Äù prompt, I intentionally exaggerated its size and adjusted the colors slightly, not too much, but enough to make it more visually pleasing.
